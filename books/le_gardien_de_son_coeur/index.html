Le Gardien De Son Coeur

Nicolas Sparks  ·  2003

2024-01-01  ·  2024-04-01

2024  ·  nice-book  ·  romance   french   drama   novel


“So bye bye, miss American Pie…”

I found this book during a visit to my cousin in Saigon. I was still learning French back then and was looking for books in French to live. This was both the first romance novel and the first book in French I’ve read.

The story follows three main characters: Julie, Mike, and Richard. Julie was a widow, Mike was a close friend of her and her late husband and Richard, a charming man that came into her life out of nowhere. It had been years since the death of her husband, but Julie had yet to recover from her loss, still living alone. Mike had had feelings for Julie for a while now, and everyone but Julie knew of this, he’s a kind man, often offerring to fix the sink, or repair Julie’s car free of charge - kind gestures that attempt to show what he’s too hesitant to express. Then, Richard came into Julie’s life, they met at the salon where Julie worked and hit it off right away. They went on a few ‘dates’, with Richard upping the ante every time, trying his best to get Julie’s heart. Mike was jealous of Richard, of course, which finally encouraged him to act on his crush on Julie. Julie was conflicted, between this new casanova that came crashing into her life and her close friend who she’s known for more than a decade, it was a very difficult choice for her. Richard wasn’t blind to Mike’s advances so he had to try harder, which eventually led to him going a bit too far and putting Julie off. This tilted the scale towards Mike, who got to get closer to Julie thanks to Richard’s misstep, little did they know however, Richard was stalking both of them. After then, using what he knows about the two of them, Richard devised a plan to break them up, by planting images of Richard and Julie getting intimate before their fallout (of which Mike was unaware) inside Julie’s house along with the pendant he gifted Julie. Predictably, Mike discovered the pictures and believed Julie was meeting Richard behind his back, which caused him to lash out at Julie and storm out of the house. Having lost both of the men she had affection for, Julie was obviously devastated, which made the perfect opportunity for Richard to get in to comfort her. Things don’t go well for Richard for too long as Julie soon discovered what Richard has done, which horrified her. She went to tell Mike who was skeptical at first but eventually trusted Julie. The two of them decide to hide in Julie’s vacation house so that Richard couldn’t stalk them while they try to find out who Richard really is. The story then turns into a mini detective drama as they uncover that Richard is actually a stolen identity, the man behind the persona stole the real Richard’s identity, what’s more is that he had an ex-wife who had the same initials as Julie’s, which partially explains his obsession with her. It doesn’t take long for “Richard” to find out where Julie is, sneaking up to the house and shooting Mike in the chest. Julie and Richard then have a standoff at the beach until Julie’s dog (whom was poisoned by Richard and was thought to be dead) came in to save the day, attacking Richard, and giving Julie an opening to attack. In the end, Richard was arrested, Julie’s dog and Mike, though gravely wounded, survive and they live happily ever after.

I realize that I skimmed over the part about Julie’s dog, which was the last gift that her late husband gave her and was supposed to be her “guardian”, hence the name of the book (oh well).

Overall, this is a pretty decent novel and it definitely gripped me, even if my French wasn’t that good then. The part where Julie discovered Richard had filmed her and Mike singing “American Pie” gave me goosebumps. The story is a rollercoaster of Julie being met with one tragedy after another and really goes to show how grief can lead one down a spiral of even more grief, and of being manipulated by others.

Since this book was translated from English, I managed to understand it quite well, barely having to look up words which is something I’ve noticed with books originally written in French and those translated to French: books translated to French has more similar words, phrases, terminology to its original language, making it easier to understand as opposed to books originally written in French.
