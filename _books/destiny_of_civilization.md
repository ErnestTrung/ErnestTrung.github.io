A truly insightful book that explains the differences between industrial capitalism (classic liberalism), finance capitalism and socialism. Surplus value and rent aren't one and the same, surplus value is created by surplus labor whilst rent is made "in the landlord's sleep". The FIRE sector (Finance, Insurance, Real Estate) attempts to extract rent that far surpass the actual surplus value that exists, creating a horridly inflated economy that will cyclically create financial crises (like that of 2008). This is what Karl Marx, Adam Smith and other classic economists warned us about. The only way to escape this system that frequently starves millions in order to enrich the fuel must be replaced by a more rational system, in fact some may say socialism or at least the fall of capitalism as we know it is inevitable.

Big recommend.
